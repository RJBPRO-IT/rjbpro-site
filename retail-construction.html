Retail Construction

Open-store work, at portfolio scale.

Retail construction doesn't happen on an empty job site. It happens in stores that are open for business, surrounded by customers, with brand standards that don't bend and timelines that don't move. RJB has been doing this work for over 30 years — and we've built the systems to execute it reliably, at scale, across any geography.

Whether you're refreshing a handful of locations or rolling out a brand update across hundreds of stores, RJB brings the same disciplined approach: detailed pre-construction planning, self-performing crews who don't need to be babysat, and communication that keeps your operations team informed at every step.

Our retail construction practice spans remodels, national rollouts, ground-up new construction, and phased programs designed around your operating calendar. We work nights and weekends so your stores stay open and your customers never notice the work was happening.

Self-Perform Advantage

Four trades. One crew. One schedule.

Most general contractors manage subcontractors. RJB self-performs paint and coatings, drywall, carpentry, and fixturing — the four core trades that make up the majority of retail interior construction. That means tighter schedules, lower costs, and no finger-pointing when the schedule gets tight.

When one company controls all four trades, the coordination happens internally — not in email chains between competing subs. The result is a faster project, a cleaner site, and a contractor who is fully accountable for every square foot of the finished space.
