For national retailers, security spend tends to flow toward the visible: cameras, monitoring, and guards. But some of the most reliable returns come from the least glamorous place on the building: the hardware on the door.

## Small upgrades, portfolio-wide impact

A three-point locking system secures a door at the top, center, and bottom of the frame rather than at a single point. The result is a door that resists forced entry, holds its alignment longer, and seals more tightly against weather and energy loss. None of that is dramatic on its own. But applied consistently across hundreds of locations, small per-door improvements compound into real savings.

## Why consistency matters

The value isn't in any single upgrade. It's in standardization across the portfolio. A managed rollout means every location is built to the same specification, every installation is documented, and facilities teams aren't troubleshooting a dozen different hardware configurations across regions.

## The RJB approach

We plan hardware programs around live-store realities: sequencing the work so it doesn't disrupt business hours, verifying each installation against spec, and reporting back so you have a clear record across every site. That's how a small change at the door becomes a measurable result across the portfolio.
